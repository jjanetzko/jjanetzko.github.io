I'm currently a basic science instructor in the Department of Molecular & Cellular Physiology at Stanford University. I work in the lab of Prof. Brian Kobilka where I use biochemical and biophysical methods to study how G protein-coupled receptors are regulated in cells. I first joined Prof. Kobilka's lab as a postdoctoral scholar in September 2017; my research has been supported by a Stanford School of Medicine Dean's Postdoctoral Fellowship, a Damon Runyon Cancer Research Foundation Fellowship and, most recently, a K99/R00 Pathway to Independence Award from the National Institute of General Medical Sciences. As a K99 fellow I am co-mentored by Prof. Mark von Zastrow (UCSF), an expert in GPCR cell biology and trafficking. In December 2022 I was appointed as an instructor in Molecular & Cellular Physiology.

Prior to coming to Stanford I earned my Ph.D. in Chemistry from Harvard University. As a graduate student I was jointly advised by Prof. Suzanne Walker and Prof. Daniel Kahne. My graduate research focussed on the essential human enzyme O-GlcNAc transferase (OGT). Specifically, my dissertation, titled: OGT Catalyzes Three Distinct Post-translational Modifications via Glycosylation centered around structure-function studies of OGT. However, I was also involved in several projects developing small molecule probes, both for OGT and for the bacterial protease ClpP.

I obtained an honours BSc from the University of Toronto in the specialist program in synthetic and catalytic chemistry. I conducted undergraduate research in organic synthesis with Prof. Robert A. Batey and organometallics with Prof. Datong Song.

Outside the lab, I am deeply involved with the local cycling community. I am an active racer on the Northern California/Nevada Cycling scene where I race for Alto Velo. Outside of my own racing, I am passionate about rider education and development. I have been a coach for the Stanford University cycling team since 2020 and was elected to serve on the Alto Velo Board of Directors in 2022 (and recently re-elected for 2023).
