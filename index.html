I'm currently a basic science instructor in the Department of Molecular & Cellular Physiology at Stanford University. I work in the lab of Prof. Brian Kobilka. I am using biochemical and biophysical methods to study how G protein-coupled receptors are regulated in cells. I joined Prof. Kobilka's lab as a postdoctoral scholar in September 2017 and my research was supported by a Stanford School of Medicine Dean's Postdoctoral Fellowship, a Damon Runyon Cancer Research Foundation Fellowship. In June 2022 I was awarded a K99/R00 Pathway to Independence Award from the National Institute of General Medical Sciences and was appointed as an instructor from December 2022.

I earned my Ph.D. in Chemistry from Harvard University, where I studied the essential human enzyme O-GlcNAc transferase (OGT). I was joint advised by Prof. Suzanne Walker, and Prof. Daniel Kahne.

Prior to that, I obtained an honours BSc from the University of Toronto in the specialist program in synthetic and catalytic chemistry. I conducted undergraduate research in organic synthesis with Prof. Robert A. Batey and organometallics with Prof. Datong Song.

Outside the lab, I am deeply involved with the local cycling community. I am an active racer on the Northern California/Nevada Cycling scene where I race for Alto Velo. Outside of my own racing, I am passionate about rider education and development. I have been a coach for the Stanford University cycling team since 2020 and was elected to serve on the Alto Velo Board of Directors in 2022. You can follow some of my adventures on two wheels here.
